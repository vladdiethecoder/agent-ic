Enterprises are about to launch thousands of AI pilots. The failure mode is not demos. It is ungoverned spend with no proof of ROI. Agent IC turns every pilot into an investment decision.

Hermes normalizes the proposal. Nemotron evaluates viability, evidence quality, and governance risk. Agent IC returns a decision, a scoped budget, an autonomous spend cap, and a risk register instead of a vague yes or no.

The agent does not get a blank check. Stripe creates a bounded authorization with proposal metadata, governance policy, and autonomous spend cap. In demo mode it is safe. With a test key it becomes a real Checkout Session.

The pilot keeps capital only by producing evidence. Agent IC tracks gross impact, spend consumed, net observed value, evidence grade, and kill criteria. At week four it can stop the pilot. By week eight it has a finance-readable continue decision.

Governance scopes every tool call: model evaluation, Hermes skills, SaaS provisioning, Stripe spend, and evidence logging. The audit log is the product: who acted, what was authorized, and what evidence changed the decision.

This is useful because CFOs and operators need a way to decide which AI pilots deserve money. It is viable because it runs locally and upgrades to NVIDIA NIM and Stripe by adding environment variables. It presents cleanly because the whole story fits in one live demo: evaluate, fund, govern, measure, decide.
