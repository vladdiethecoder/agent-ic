Agents can now spend money and provision tools. That is powerful, and dangerous. Agent IC gives agents a governed capital account.

Here the mission is to resolve one hundred late-freight exceptions in seventy-two hours. Before work starts, Agent IC creates a one-hundred-dollar spend envelope, approved tools, and kill criteria.

Stripe records the bounded authorization. This is not a blank check. The spend path carries the proposal ID, policy, cap, and renewal rules. The Stripe Link skill can buy a fifteen-dollar SaaS subscription with a one-time card, but only after human approval.

Now the agent tries to buy a one-hundred-fifty-dollar premium lookup API. NemoClaw's OpenShell sandbox intercepts the call and returns four-oh-three Forbidden, because the merchant is outside the approved list and the cap is exceeded. No money moves.

Then Agent IC imports evidence: one hundred cases processed, forty-eight auto-triaged, ninety-one percent QA agreement, six hundred thirty-one dollars net value, zero critical incidents.

The decision is continue, with a two-hundred-fifty-dollar next cap and draft-only autonomy. Hermes saves the whole procedure as a reusable playbook.

Lower the QA threshold to eighty-two percent, and the decision flips to kill. This is how enterprises let agents operate with money without losing control.
