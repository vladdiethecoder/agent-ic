Agent IC is an enterprise procurement control plane for agentic spend. It gives finance, security, and operations one governed way to decide which AI vendors earn budget, tools, data, and production access.

Instead of approving a vendor agent on trust, Agent IC funds a bounded trial, enforces policy, measures evidence, validates claims, and carries the result into renewals.

RouteGuard AI is one example. Before a fourteen point four K contract, Agent IC gives the trial a one-hundred-dollar Stripe test-mode cap and a governed workload.

The decision is not just yes or no. Safe trial continues, but a one-hundred-fifty-dollar CARFAX enrichment is blocked by NVIDIA OpenShell because it exceeds the approved cap.

Now the buyer sees value: 301 complaints auto-routed, 29 human-review cases, two thousand six hundred sixty-nine dollars net value, and six point one eight times risk-adjusted ROI.

The proof remains attached to the decision: Nemotron classifies 3 sample complaints, 327 pattern-extended rows stay labeled, plus Stripe test-mode receipt, NHTSA hash, OpenShell 403, and Hermes CLI receipt.

That is the enterprise product. Every agent gets the same trial, policy enforcement, ROI proof, and renewal evidence before spend or production access expands.
